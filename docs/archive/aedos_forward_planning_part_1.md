## 0. The frame: the delta between the architecture we built and the architecture we need

You named the right frame twice in your response: *assess what the project really is, whether the architecture we built is what we want, and identify the delta versus the architecture we actually need.* This document is organized around that delta.

The synthesis established **what we built**: a sound, sophisticated, largely *dormant* per-claim verification engine. Your response established **what we want**: a system that makes durable progress on a structural class of LLM failure, by reasoning over Wikidata facts and user assertions to know more than either states alone — with no hardcoding, every claim verified, and an elegant substrate that discovers relationships in the connected data rather than having them hand-fed.

The literature lets me sharpen **what we need** to close that delta, and — importantly — to tell you which parts of the gap are *bounded engineering*, which require *significant architectural change*, and which are *hard external ceilings* that no architecture escapes. That three-way classification is the spine of this document, because it is the difference between "tune to oblivion" (which we agree is the wrong move) and "challenge the architecture to surpass its ceilings" (which is the right one).

A single finding reframes the whole forward plan, so I put it first: **the multi-source-derivation problem you flagged as the central weakness is not where you think it is, and the fix the field has converged on is one Aedos has already half-built.** More on that in §3.1. But the meta-point is that the review was worth doing precisely because "close enough" development left us unsure where the real architectural cuts are — and several of them are different from what the surface symptoms suggested.

## 1. What Aedos is for — and the honest, literature-backed case for it

You pushed back hard on the synthesis's "deployment context shifted / hallucination absorbed by calibration" framing, and you are substantially right. Let me restate the position precisely, because the literature both vindicates your core claim and sharpens it into something more defensible and more *measurable* than either of us had.

**Your claim — hallucination is structural and permanent — is correct, and there are two distinct, formally-proven floors.** These are not the same mechanism, and Aedos's three-tier design happens to map onto exactly the two of them that matter:

1. **The calibration / singleton-rate floor.** Kalai & Vempala, *Calibrated Language Models Must Hallucinate* (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.14648), and the OpenAI follow-up *Why Language Models Hallucinate* (https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04664), prove that a statistically *calibrated* LM **must** hallucinate on arbitrary facts at a rate lower-bounded by the fraction of facts seen exactly once in training (a Good-Turing / missing-mass argument). If 20% of birthdays appear once in training, expect ≥20% error on birthday questions — and this is *inescapable by scaling data or improving the model*, so long as the model stays calibrated. The only escapes are an **external grounded source** or **abstention**. That is Aedos's KB tier + abstention discipline, derived from first principles. This is the strongest citation for the whole project and it should anchor the v0.16 justification — not the weaker "hallucination is inevitable" existence proof (Xu et al., https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.11817), which is true but operationally vacuous (it proves *infinitely many* wrong inputs exist, not a *rate*, and Suzuki et al. https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12187 rebut it as unable to explain practical hallucination).

2. **The architectural / TC⁰ floor (math, counting, character-level, citation).** A fixed-depth Transformer in a single forward pass sits in complexity class ~TC⁰; inductive counting and carry-propagating arithmetic are provably not in TC⁰, so a one-shot answer *cannot in general be computed* (*Counting Ability of LLMs*, https://arxiv.org/html/2410.19730v2; *The Lookahead Limitation*, https://arxiv.org/pdf/2502.19981). Tokenization compounds it (the model never sees characters). This is exactly your "count the vowels / days with the letter d" example, and it is **architecturally permanent for any forward pass**. The only sound escape is deterministic external computation — which PAL (https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2211.10435) demonstrated cleanly: on GSM-Hard, offloading to a Python interpreter held accuracy at 61% while chain-of-thought collapsed to 23%. That is a direct precedent for, and vindication of, Aedos's **Python tier**. The implication is a hard routing rule: *any quantitative or character-level claim must route to Python — never to the LLM and never to the KB.*

So the synthesis was wrong to imply calibration "absorbed" the problem. The correct statement, which you should adopt because it is both true and *defensible against the obvious objection*, is: **hallucination is permanent and structural, and Aedos's durable value lives precisely where the two theoretical floors bite — the long tail of arbitrary facts (KB grounding + abstention) and math/computation (Python).** What a calibrated baseline *has* largely solved is the *easy* middle: popular, head-entity factoids. On those, Aedos adds little and — because Wikidata's coverage is imperfect — can even net-lose by abstaining where the bare LLM was right. This is not a weakness in the thesis; it is the thesis correctly scoped.

**The honest reframing of "what Aedos does," which I'd ask you to accept:** Aedos performs **failure-mode transformation**, not hallucination elimination. It converts a silent, confident error into either a grounded verdict or an explicit abstention. It cannot eliminate hallucination — its own extractor and oracles are LLMs and inherit the same floors (this is the unavoidable consequence of Xu's theorem applied to Aedos itself, and the forward plan must treat oracle output as fallible-and-quarantined, never as ground truth). But changing the *failure mode* from "confidently wrong" to "honestly abstains or grounds" is exactly the property that matters in the deployments you care about, and it is the property no better LLM provides for itself.

This reframing also resolves your skepticism about the principled-abstention "win" ("100% vs 20% — it basically abstains on everything"). You're right that an abstain-on-everything system trivially wins a benchmark whose right answer is "abstain." The win is real but the *operating point* is too conservative — and that is a fixable consequence of two specific defects this plan addresses: the extractor silently dropping claims (§3.4) and the composition engine failing to ground chains it should (§3.1). Fix those and the operating point moves from "abstains on everything it can't trivially look up" toward "abstains only where grounding genuinely fails" — which is where the soundness/coverage frontier should sit.

**The closest architectural precedent confirms both the bet and its cost.** LINC (https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2310.15164) — LLM-as-semantic-parser to a symbolic solver — exhibits exactly Aedos's profile: ~93% precision but ~60% recall, and it outputs "Uncertain" (its abstention) 57% of the time. Symbolic offload *trades coverage for precision*, and abstention becomes the dominant output. This is honest precedent that Aedos's bet is real and that its coverage will be structurally low — not a bug to tune away but the signature of the design. SAFE (https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.18802), the closest sibling to Aedos's shape, makes the epistemics explicit in a way Aedos should copy verbatim: its verdicts mean "supported *by Google Search*," not "globally true," and it concedes there are infinitely many true statements its source can't locate. Aedos's verdicts are "supported by Tier U / Wikidata / computation," and unverifiable ≠ false. That framing is the cleanest published justification for soundness-over-coverage.

## 2. The baseline false-verified discrepancy, resolved

You asked why the LLM baseline's false-verified rate is 9.8–15.6% on the curated medium-bar set but ~0% on SimpleQA/PopQA, and whether it's contamination or query formation. It is **three stacked factors**, and the answer matters because it tells us what the medium-bar actually measures and where Aedos's value is.

**Factor 1 — task framing (the dominant one, and it's query formation, confirmed in code).** The two baselines run different tasks. The medium-bar `BaselineRunner` (`benchmark.py:254`) hands the model a *declarative statement* and asks it to classify "VERIFIED / CONTRADICTED / ABSTAIN" with an empty system prompt. The benchmark `baseline_runner.py` hands it an *open question* with a system prompt that explicitly says "if you don't know, say so… do not fabricate." Presenting a plausible-sounding false statement and asking a model to judge it invites *endorsement* (acquiescence to the presupposition); asking an open long-tail question invites *abstention*. The KG-FPQ false-premise study (https://arxiv.org/html/2407.05868) quantifies the format sensitivity: true-premise accuracy ~61–71% vs false-premise ~48–55%. The medium-bar deliberately seeds 14 contradicted + 24 abstain ground-truth cases; the baseline's false-verifieds are overwhelmingly it endorsing the seeded contradicted statements.

**Factor 2 — difficulty selection × the calibration boundary.** Per the OpenAI SimpleQA paper (https://arxiv.org/html/2411.04368v1), frontier models get *most* SimpleQA questions wrong but a calibrated model converts would-be-errors into *abstentions* (Claude-3.5-Sonnet: 35% not-attempted). A "~0% hallucination" baseline on SimpleQA/PopQA is abstaining on the head-and-tail it doesn't know, not recalling correctly. The medium-bar is curated to be tail/compositional, which pushes the model past its abstention boundary into confident error.

**Factor 3 — contamination (secondary).** SimpleQA/PopQA are public; Inference-Time Decontamination (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.13990) removed ~19–23% of apparent accuracy on standard benchmarks. A fresh medium-bar set is less contaminated, so it reports truer (lower) generalization. But contamination inflates *correct-when-attempted*, not the false-verified rate directly, so it's a minor contributor to *this* gap.

**The implication that matters for the forward plan:** the medium-bar is, in effect, a *near-false-premise / low-popularity probe* — exactly the regime where verification has value — while SimpleQA/PopQA partly measure memorization + abstention. So **Aedos's "we beat the baseline on false-verified" is measured on the statement-classification task, whereas the deployment task is open-draft generation.** That's not a flaw in the result; it means the right way to measure Aedos's value (deferred to the eval-building work, but worth stating now) is to stratify by entity popularity (PopQA-style buckets) and report lift *per bucket* — expecting ≈0 on head facts and real lift in the tail. This converts your structural-hallucination argument from a stance into a measurable claim. It also tells us the medium-bar's higher baseline error is the *honest* signal, and the right baseline number to hold in mind for Aedos's value proposition.

## 3. The architecture we need

Seven problems, ordered by your stated priorities. For each: the precise current behavior (grounded in code), what the literature says the fix is, the design options with trade-offs, and an explicit verdict on whether the change is **bounded engineering**, a **significant architectural cut**, or **gated by a hard external ceiling**.

### 3.1 Multi-source derivation — the central problem, reframed

This is the one you most want solved and are most open to brainstorming on, so it gets the most space. The reframing is the most important single finding in this document.

**There are two composition mechanisms in Aedos today, and they have opposite profiles.** This is the crux, and the surface symptoms conflate them:

- **Mechanism A — the walker's BFS frontier** (`walker.py:328-386`). Hop-by-hop, slot-substitution, budget-bounded, gated by the `predicate_distribution` oracle, with the live-KB-neighbor fallback capped to `depth == 0`. **This is the weak one** — the ~54% derivation, the 0–45% multi-hop, the "distribution gate closes," the "can't do 2+ hops."
- **Mechanism B — the KB-verifier's internal SPARQL property-path ASK** (`kb_verifier.py:_subsumption_upgrades`; `kb_wikidata.py:_build_subsumption_ask_query:324-365`). When a KB lookup value mismatches, it fires `ASK { wd:source (wdt:P131|wdt:P30|wdt:P17)+ wd:target }` — a transitive path query that verifies a whole multi-hop chain in **one round-trip**, soundly. **This already works.** "Paris located_in France ⇒ Paris located_in Europe" passes via Mechanism B, never touching the walker frontier or the distribution gate.

So the walker's BFS is largely a slower, worse re-implementation of what a SPARQL property-path already does for the relation family where multi-hop matters most. The case that genuinely *needs* the walker — "Asa lives_in Williamstown (Tier U); Williamstown ⊂ Massachusetts ⊂ USA (KB) ⇒ Asa lives_in USA" — fails for three diagnosable reasons (all confirmed in code): (1) the cold substrate has no cached subsumption rows and `find_neighbors` reads *only* local rows (`subsumption.py:161-204`), never the KB; (2) the live fallback descends from USA's *millions* of incoming edges with a blind 20-sample (wrong direction, unbounded end); (3) the `depth == 0` cap forbids the iterative narrowing the chain needs. The walker is searching the wrong direction with the wrong mechanism.

**What the literature says.** The KGQA field — over exactly Aedos's setting (Wikidata/Freebase, multi-hop) — has *abandoned* uniform frontier-expansion BFS, for two empirically-demonstrated reasons: bigger frontier = *worse* answers (Subgraph-Retrieval shows accuracy crashes past ~5,000 nodes, https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.13296), and the hard part is *selecting* which relation to expand (StructGPT's error analysis: **74% of errors are relation/entity selection**, only ~5% hallucination, https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.09645). The convergent winning pattern is **plan-then-verify**: predict the relation path first, then instantiate/verify it deterministically. Reasoning-on-Graphs (RoG, https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01061) generates faithful relation paths then verifies them — ablating the planner collapses F1 from 70.8 to 49.7. Plan-on-Graph (PoG, https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.23875) adds Memory + Reflection + **backtracking** and beats iterative search on both accuracy *and* cost (41% fewer LLM calls). StructGPT's KB interfaces — `Extract_Neighbor_Relations(e)`, `Extract_Triples(e, {r})` — are almost identical to Aedos's `enumerate_neighbors` / `lookup_statements`; Aedos already has the right primitives and lacks only the relation-relevance ranking on top of them.

**The design — four moves, in order of leverage:**

1. **Promote SPARQL property-paths to a first-class composition primitive (the proven Mechanism B, generalized).** For any claim whose predicate maps to a transitive KB property, verify the multi-hop in one ASK instead of hop-by-hop BFS. This is the highest-leverage move because the codebase already proves it works; it just needs to be lifted out of the KB-verifier's geographic special-case and made general. *Bounded* — the lookup/path primitives exist.

2. **Add forward-from-premise / bidirectional search.** The Williamstown case fails because the walker descends from USA (unbounded incoming edges) instead of *ascending* from the known premise Williamstown (a handful of bounded outgoing P131 edges: Williamstown → Berkshire County → Massachusetts → USA). Seed a premise frontier from the session's Tier U facts, expand it forward via *outgoing* KB edges (which `_build_neighbors_query` already supports un-LIMIT'd, `kb_wikidata.py:246-254`), and meet the goal frontier in the middle. **This directly defuses the `depth == 0` cap** (the cap exists only because incoming-edge enumeration is unbounded; outgoing is naturally bounded) and is the natural home for tri-source composition. *Bounded → moderate.* Strongly recommended.

3. **Separate chain DISCOVERY from chain VERIFICATION — the cleanest reframe and the spine of the redesign.** Today the two are fused: `_expand_via_substrate` both *proposes* a substitution and (next iteration) *verifies* it, and the `predicate_distribution` gate is a soundness check applied at *discovery* time (`walker.py:944` `if not directions: continue`). That fusion is *why* a sound chain the oracle mislabels "neither" is never even tried — the gate closing is a false-abstain generator. Let discovery be liberal and unsound (propose any plausible chain — via SPARQL paths, KB neighbors, or an LLM plan), and make **verification the sole soundness authority** (does this concrete chain hold, edge by edge, against Tier U / KB / Python?). This strictly improves soundness (§3.2 is enforced where it belongs, at verification) *and* raises coverage (discovery stops pre-filtering). It is also exactly the field's plan-then-verify consensus, and it lets you adopt PoG-style backtracking as the principled way to *wake the dormant truth-maintenance pillar* — within-derivation reflection/backtracking, not a global belief web (see §3.6). *Significant but bounded — it relocates where the soundness check sits; the sources are untouched.*

4. **Split the `predicate_distribution` oracle.** It is doing double duty and is the wrong abstraction as a gate, for three reasons: its prompt *is* a hand-curated map (the hardcoding you want gone, `predicate_distribution.py:158-195`); it operates at relation-*family* granularity ("part_of") when transitivity is property-*specific*; and it gates at discovery time. Split it into (i) **KB-property transitivity** — discoverable from the graph and from Wikidata's own property constraints, *no LLM opinion needed* (transitivity of P131 is a graph property, established by the `+` path operator) — and (ii) **intensional entailment over kinds** (the `mortal`/`is_a`/distributes-down semantics that genuinely needs a judgment and has no SPARQL analogue). Conflating them under one "distribution verdict" is the design smell. *Bounded.*

**The TMS connection (engaging your Doyle/de Kleer lineage).** A full eager truth-maintenance system is the *wrong* model for Aedos, and the literature is unambiguous about why (Area-1 research): monolithic JTMS/ATMS died of label explosion (exponential provenance maintenance), odd-loop non-termination, and order-sensitivity — and a session-scoped per-claim verifier has *no long-lived belief state for the maintenance machinery to amortize over*, which is precisely why the pillar is dormant: **it has no job.** What you want instead is the Doyle/de Kleer *data structure* (the justification/dependency graph), computed **lazily per claim and discarded per session** — which is mathematically the **semiring provenance polynomial** from the database literature (Green/Tannen, https://homepages.inf.ed.ac.uk/jcheney/publications/provdbsurvey.pdf): `⊗` for the conjunction of hops/sources a derivation needs, `⊕` for alternative derivations. This single representation delivers three things you want at once: the auditable composition trace, the generalization of `*_given_assertion` (it's just "the provenance term contains a Tier-U assumption variable"), and the corrected-value extraction (§3.5). PoG's backtracking is the within-derivation version of retraction; that is the only "truth maintenance" a session-scoped verifier needs.

**True architectural blockers here:**
- **Cross-source chains can never collapse to one SPARQL query** — Wikidata knows nothing of session personas (Tier U) or computed facts (Python). An orchestrator above SPARQL is *permanent*; the tri-source composition survives every redesign. The walker's role is real; only its mechanism (discover/verify + bidirectional) should change.
- **Wikidata transitivity is property-specific and leaky** — the Marie-Curie / Warsaw-⊄-Germany leak (P361/P206 are not entailment-preserving for `born_in`, `kb_wikidata.py:143-178`) is the cautionary tale. KB-chain soundness is bounded by *which property paths preserve entailment for which predicate* — a partly-curatorial fact. This is the **one place "no hardcoding" hits a genuine wall**: the system must at minimum *discover and then trust* a transitivity fact, and discovery can be wrong (§3.7).
- **Multi-hop accuracy degrades intrinsically past ~3 hops** (HoVer shows monotonic decay, https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.03088; error compounds multiplicatively). A better engine moves multi-hop from 0–45% toward the **60–85% band** the agent methods occupy — but cannot reach single-hop accuracy at depth, and **cannot exceed that band without either KG link-prediction (which sacrifices the provenance Aedos's whole thesis requires) or additional grounding sources.** This is the honest ceiling on "reason over Wikidata to know more than it states": the gain is bounded by what is *derivable* from present facts, not by what can be *invented*.

### 3.2 The substrate as a predicate map — multi-property bindings and Wikidata's own ontology

This addresses normalization.py (which you want gone), the P31-vs-P106 gap, your "discover similar predicates / build a map automatically" instinct, your dislike of seeds, and the new-predicate problem. They are one problem.

**The P31-vs-P106 bug has two root causes at two layers.** `normalization.py:60-65` freezes "is a" → `instance_of` → P31 *before the object is even examined*, and the `predicate_translation` oracle then commits to a single scalar `kb_property`. A natural-language copula ("X is a physicist") is genuinely ambiguous across P31 (type), P106 (occupation), P39 (position), P101 (field) depending on the object's type — and the architecture chooses one property before looking at the object. **So removing the hardcoded map without fixing the mapping logic merely surfaces the bug; it doesn't fix it.**

**The decisive, under-exploited fact:** Wikidata encodes its *own* property relationships, and Aedos uses *none* of them (a grep for these P-numbers across `src/` returns zero hits):
- **P2302 property constraints** — subject-type and value-type constraints. P106's value-type constraint says its value must be a subclass of occupation/profession; its subject-type says the subject must be human (Q5). **This makes the copula choice a lookup, not a guess**: "X is a physicist" — physicist (Q169470) is-a profession, X is human ⇒ the value-type constraint on P106 matches and P31's does not ⇒ consult P106. This is the principled, cold-start, no-hardcoding replacement for the prompt's hand-written "occupation P106" line.
- **P1696 inverse-property** — *auto-derives* the inverse slot maps the seed pack hand-writes today (capital_of/has_capital, mother_of/parent_of). No more LLM-guessing `{subject: statement_value}`.
- **P1647 subproperty-of / P1659 related-property / P1628 equivalent-property** — a near-free, hand-curation-free *predicate-relatedness graph* read directly from Wikidata, exactly the "map of the predicates in the connected data" you described. (Caveat: P1647 is advisory — the Wikidata community explicitly declined OWL/DL semantics — so it's a retrieval prior, not logical entailment; it still needs validation.)

**The literature confirms the method shape.** Falcon 2.0 (https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.11270) proves pure alias-lookup relation linking caps at ~0.40 F1 — which is what Aedos's oracle does today (free-recall a P-number from a prompt). GenRL (https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.07337) reaches 0.84 on clean Wikidata by **retrieve-then-rerank-then-validate**: feed the model the *real* candidate properties (the ones that actually appear on the resolved subject's statements, plus label-similar ones), make it *re-rank* rather than free-recall, and *validate* the pick with a live SPARQL ASK before trusting it. SLING (https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.07726) shows you can build the surface-form→property map *cold, with zero hand-seeds*, via distant supervision from Wikidata itself. LLMs4OM (https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.10317) gives the retrieve-then-LLM-judge pattern for discovering *relatedness* between properties — but with the universal warning that hallucinated matches are the #1 error, so every auto-discovered edge must be structurally validated.

**The design:**
- **Make `kb_property` a ranked candidate *set*, not a scalar**, and add an **evidence-arbitration** step to `kb_verifier.verify`: loop the candidates, look up each, and the property whose statement value matches the resolved object (and whose P2302 constraints the entities satisfy) wins. VERIFIED only on a positive value-match; CONTRADICTED gated to the single-valued, constraint-matching property — so fanning out across properties never fabricates a contradiction.
- **Thread the object's resolved type into `predicate_translation.consult`** (currently predicate-string-only) so copula ambiguity is resolved against the data.
- **Build a cached property-relations graph** from P2302/P1647/P1696/P1659 using the existing cache-then-generate substrate pattern, mirroring how `predicate_translation` already works.
- **Delete `normalization.py`'s `_CANONICAL_MAP` and the ~50 synonym alias seed rows.** Roughly 50 of the 83 seed entries are pure synonyms ("works_at"/"employed_by"/"works_for" → P108) — a hardcoded normalization table in disguise. They vanish once every surface form resolves to a P-id via the ontology path. Target: **seeds become optional operator overrides, empty by default** — which is the cold-start ideal you want.

**Engaging your seeds-vs-cold-start tension directly:** you're right to prefer cold-start, and the ontology path makes it *more* achievable than today, not less — because synonym-collapsing and inverse-derivation move from hand-curation to Wikidata lookups. The new-predicate problem is *better* handled by this design than by seeds: a never-seen predicate triggers candidate-property retrieval + constraint pruning + evidence arbitration, which structurally catches datatype mismatches (the P585-vs-P50 error class) instead of silently mis-mapping. Embedding-based relation similarity (RotatE, https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10197) is *not* the answer for the new-predicate case — it gives garbage vectors for rare/new properties and conflates co-occurrence with equivalence (P31 and P106 sit *near* each other precisely because they co-apply, the wrong signal). Use the oracle's top-k + constraint pruning + live validation instead.

**True architectural blockers here:**
- **Single-property → multi-property bindings is the one unavoidable schema cut.** `PredicateMetadata.kb_property` is scalar (`predicate_translation.py:258`), the DB column is scalar, `kb_verifier` does one lookup (`:217`), and `_lookup_targets` returns one direction. Every discovery option requires a *set* of `(property, direction, slot_map, constraints)` bindings and an arbitration loop. This is invasive but the lookup/resolve/compare primitives all already exist — what changes is they run in a loop with a new arbitration function. Soundness is preservable.
- **Predicate identity is frozen at extraction before the object is seen.** Resolving copula ambiguity requires *deferring property selection past extraction* — a cross-layer interface change. Bounded but real.
- **The honest accuracy ceiling is ~0.85** on common predicates over clean Wikidata, and 0.48–0.65 on hard/implicit/multi-relation cases (evidence-backed across Falcon/GenRL/SLING, not a tuning gap). Soundness-over-coverage — abstain on low-confidence picks — is the correct response to this ceiling, which is why the architecture's existing stance is right.

### 3.3 Granular claims and the temporal model

You questioned whether the current temporal model is the right architectural decision, and proposed treating "X started at T1" and "X ended at T2" as separate claims, reasoning over their *existence* to decide if a condition holds over time. The literature says your instinct is correct *and* names the exact formal tool.

**On granularity generally:** Molecular Facts (https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.20079) *validates* Aedos's (subject, predicate, object) triple as the right atomic grain — going finer loses the entity context the KB needs to resolve the right QID (the "Ann Jansson" wrong-entity failure). But it adds a crucial refinement that also feeds §3.2: the subject/object must carry *just enough* disambiguation to resolve (decontextuality + minimality) — and the disambiguator a person-claim needs ("physicist" as an *occupation type*) is *exactly* what P106 encodes. So the granularity question and the predicate-map question are the same question viewed from two sides. The countervailing warning (Decomposition Dilemmas, https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.02400): *over*-decomposition lowers accuracy — keep sub-claims ≤ input complexity and add a reflection pass. Your "every claim verified" goal is right, but splitting one sentence into five atoms manufactures false abstains.

**On temporal specifically — your "reason over the existence of start/end claims" is precisely the pointizable (convex) fragment of Allen's interval algebra.** Full Allen interval algebra is NP-complete (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allen's_interval_algebra), but the fragment expressible as endpoint inequalities — point algebra over start/end points with `<, =, >` — is **polynomial**. Model each condition's validity as a typed interval `{start: TimePoint|unknown|open, end: TimePoint|unknown|open}`; your "start claim" and "end claim" populate the two endpoints; "holds at T" is `start ≤ T ≤ end` under three-valued logic for unknown endpoints (which subsumes the current `BEFORE_PRESENT` sentinel cleanly as "end = open-before-now"). The decisive negative result you must respect: CronKGQA (https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.01515) proves embedding/LLM-similarity **fails** on before/after/first/last — you *cannot* let the LLM "feel" temporal order; you **must** implement an explicit symbolic timepoint comparator (EXAQT's pipeline: normalize all dates to one timeline → compute interval relations symbolically → compose, https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.08935). And TNTComplEx (https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.04926) suggests a clean addition to the substrate: a *data-discovered temporal-volatility flag* per predicate (time-varying like `employed_by` vs time-invariant like `mother_of`), so interval reasoning runs only on volatile predicates.

**The design (Option T1, the bounded first step):** split fused predicates — instead of `employed_by(Asa, Google, valid_from=2020, valid_until=2024)`, emit `employment_started(Asa, Google, 2020)` and `employment_ended(Asa, Google, 2024)` as independent claims, each grounded against KB qualifiers, with a small "interval-from-events" resolver answering "holds at T?" by claim-existence + boundary arithmetic. This **reuses the existing `born_on`/`died_on` → P569/P570 date-in-object precedent** (`extractor.py:425-443`) rather than inventing a new shape, and needs *no* new Claim fields and *no* Allen algebra. A natural co-change is adding `valid_from_ref`/`valid_until_ref` to fix the Rule-16 overload (the prompt already generates the event ids; the model just can't store the distinction).

**True blockers here are external (Wikidata's data model), not Aedos's architecture:**
- Wikidata stores start/end as **qualifiers** (P580/P582) on a statement, not as standalone statements — so granular start/end grounding depends on the KB adapter *surfacing qualifiers* off a statement (a protocol capability the adapter mostly has but the walker doesn't fully use). This is the true constraint.
- Wikidata has **no native event-relative time** ("valid until he resigned") — storable in Tier U / Aedos's own claim graph but *ungroundable against Wikidata* until the bounding event resolves to a date. Intrinsic data-model wall.
- Day-max precision; Julian/Gregorian normalization needed pre-1583 (naive string comparison across the 1583 boundary is unsound).

### 3.4 Verify every claim — eliminate drop-to-abstain

You called the silent dropping a huge finding and insisted every claim must be verified (abstention is a verdict, not a pre-filter). The literature agrees this is the right invariant, and the fix is **bounded, not architectural.**

There are exactly four places `_build_claim` returns `None` for a *shaped* claim — hard-claim substring check, content-less event, `subject == object`, `predicate == object` — plus a fifth implicit drop (the `INERT_PROSE` triage filter). The cleanest fix: `_build_claim` never returns `None` for a shaped claim; instead it stamps an `abstention_reason` (`self_referential` / `predicate_eq_object` / `content_less_event` / `subject_absent_from_source`) that the walker short-circuits to `no_grounding_found` *before any KB lookup*. The aggregator already buckets `no_grounding_found → abstained`, so nothing in the six-verdict machinery breaks; a single new `abstention_reason` enum-space suffices (no new top-level verdict needed).

Three things to get right (confirmed in code):
- **Filter B (content-less event) is already obsolete** — it existed to stop a stray claim from dragging a *compound* verdict to abstain, but the Session-2 per-claim intervention redesign already isolates per-claim verdicts, so the regression it guards cannot recur. Delete it outright; lowest risk.
- **Filters C/D (self-reference, predicate-echo) must become *pre-walk* abstains, not "let it walk"** — if `(Einstein, born_in, Einstein)` reaches the KB lookup it produces a §3.2 false-contradiction. The soundness the drops currently buy is preserved only by short-circuiting before lookup.
- **Filter A (hard-claim substring) detection must be preserved** — deleting it lets hallucinated-subject claims get KB-verified. Convert it to `no_grounding_found(subject_absent_from_source)`, not a silent drop.

The literature frames this as **"designation, not deletion"**: SAFE's relevance *filter* (which drops) is the anti-pattern for Aedos; the fix is that every extracted unit gets a verdict including "not-checkworthy." And SAFE's **F1@K** metric (https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.18802) is what creates *architectural pressure* against dropping — it adds a recall term so verifying only the easy claims costs you. Aedos lacks any recall-bearing metric; adding one (deferred to eval work) is what would keep the no-drop invariant honest. This also directly raises the principled-abstention operating point you were skeptical of: fewer silent drops → fewer trivial abstentions → the abstain rate reflects genuine grounding failure, not extractor fastidiousness.

**Engaging your point about the prompt:** you noted the 24-rule prompt isn't bad but can never contain enough rules — it will always be thin somewhere and can't grow indefinitely. This is correct and it's an argument *for* §3.2's direction: knowledge that currently lives as accreting prompt rules (which predicate a copula maps to, which surface forms are synonyms) should move to *Wikidata's own ontology + live evidence*, where it scales without prompt growth. The prompt's job shrinks to "produce a well-formed (s,p,o) with enough disambiguation"; the *mapping* knowledge moves to the substrate-as-predicate-map. The four drop-filters are themselves prompt-compensation band-aids ("until the extractor reliably applies Rule 15…"); converting them to abstentions removes the pressure to keep patching the prompt.

### 3.5 Per-claim correction (emit the value) and simplifying Layer 5

You want corrections to edit the claim / emit the corrected value, and you found Layer 5 over-complicated. Both are addressed by a small, bounded change plus a simplification.

**The corrected value is already computed and thrown away.** On a contradiction, `KBVerifier._compare_positive` returns the conflicting KB statement; its `.value` *is* the corrected value. But the walker's contradiction branch (`walker.py:698-704`) builds a `grounding` dict and **drops `matched_statement.value` one line before it could be plumbed.** The fix is ~3 edits: carry `contradicting_value` onto the trace edge; add a `contradicting_value` field to `ClaimVerdict` (the docstring already names it as deferred); and enrich `_format_correction` to emit "…the source indicates {value} instead." Aedos is actually *better-positioned than RARR* (https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.08726, the reference correction system, which needs a free-text edit model) because Aedos already holds the ground-truth value — it can do a minimal deterministic edit (swap the object, keep subject+predicate), which is exactly RARR's *preservation* discipline (don't rewrite the user's content; RARR scored 89.6 preservation vs a free-editing baseline's 39.1). Caveat: entity-valued corrections are Q-ids needing a reverse-label lookup (one extra KB call); dates/quantities are already readable. Bounded, not a blocker.

**On simplifying Layer 5 / "pass the per-claim issue to an aggregator model":** you're right that the value proposition is per-claim and the response composition is somewhat beside the LM-vs-Aedos thesis. The honest division is: Aedos's *contribution* is the per-claim verdict + the corrected value + the grounding source; *how* that gets woven into a user-facing response (rewrite the draft, annotate it, or hand the per-claim deltas to a composer model) is a presentation layer that can be as simple as emitting the structured per-claim findings. The literature supports keeping verdicts per-claim and aggregating only for a coverage summary (FActScore/SAFE/RARR are all per-fact). ALCE (https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14627) offers the rigorous definition to adopt for what "verified" *means*: the grounding set must *entail* the claim (recall) and every cited premise must be *necessary* (precision) — a sharper, checkable spec for `verified` and `*_given_assertion` than the current informal "a source was found."

### 3.6 Dead and dormant code — wire or remove

You said dead code should be investigated and either removed (from repo *and* docs) or implemented if dead-in-error. Three items, with verdicts:

- **Layer 2 Router/Validator — dead-in-design, not dead-in-error.** The walker subsumed routing deliberately (it reads `routing_hint` inline via `_predicate_routing`). Two clean options: **(i) remove** Router+Validator and update `architecture.md` to say routing is a walker concern; or **(ii) resurrect the Validator's `distinct_slots`/`object_type` checks as the *principled* home for the extractor's hardcoded `subject==object`/`predicate==object` drops** (filters C/D from §3.4). Option (ii) is attractive precisely because of your no-hardcoding thesis: it turns the casefold-hack drops into typed structural invariants that emit an anomaly→abstain. The Router-as-router is redundant either way and should go.
- **The retraction pillar — "worst of both: cost without function."** The dependency *index* is wired and populated in production (the aggregator records verdict traces every turn), but the *cascade* is unimplemented (`propagate_retraction`'s return value is discarded), `ContradictionTracer` is never constructed in production, and **KB-grounded verdicts carry no retractable row id so can never be retracted at all.** So you pay an audit-log write per verdict for a mechanism that never fires. The fork is genuine and you should decide it explicitly:
  - **If over-time truth-maintenance is a v0.16 commitment:** implement it the *right* way per §3.1 and the TMS research — lazy per-claim provenance, bounded within-session nogood caching, and IVM-style delta-propagation **scoped to the one retraction Aedos actually needs** (the user changes/retracts a premise mid-session), with PoG-style within-derivation backtracking. *Not* a global eager belief web (which the literature shows is exponential, order-sensitive, and has no workload to amortize in a session-scoped verifier).
  - **If it isn't:** remove the propagator/tracer/replay machinery and delete §7.3 from `architecture.md`. The current half-built state is the only clearly-wrong option.
  
  My read: the genuinely useful, bounded version is "user corrects a premise → re-derive the affected in-flight claims." That is worth having and is cheap under the provenance model. The full self-healing belief graph is not worth building for a session-scoped verifier. So: *keep a minimal premise-retraction, remove the cascade ambition, rewrite §7.3 to match.*
- **Collapsed dual-designation — observability-only, near-vestigial.** The `*_given_assertion` verdicts are computed then collapsed by `base_verdict_of` before any user-facing use, so the distinction is invisible to users. Decide: **surface it** (a bounded ~10-line change to emit "this is true *if your premise holds*" — which is genuinely valuable for the session-knowledge use case and is the cheapest way to express premise-dependent verdicts), or **fold it into a trace flag** and stop carrying three extra verdict strings. Given your "who is Aedos for — all three, if it works" answer, surfacing conditional verdicts is probably worth it.

### 3.7 The geographic disaster and the one place "no hardcoding" hits a wall

You called the geographic handling a disaster and want an elegant, substrate-based solution. Most of it dissolves into §3.1 and §3.2: the `CONTINENT_QIDS` table and `_GEO_REGION_TYPES` set are special-cased hardcodings that the property-transitivity discovery (§3.1.4) + Wikidata's own property constraints (§3.2) subsume — geographic containment is just "P131/P30/P17 is transitive," which the SPARQL `+` path establishes from the graph, no hand-table needed.

**But this is also where I have to be honest with you about a limit of the no-hardcoding thesis.** The Marie-Curie leak (Warsaw → P206 Vistula → P17 → "Germany" produced a false `born_in`) is *why* the property set was trimmed and type-guarded. The lesson is that **Wikidata transitivity is property- and predicate-specific and sometimes leaky**: P361 ("part of") is entailment-preserving for some predicates and not others, and no amount of search cleverness makes an untrustworthy path trustworthy. So the system must, at minimum, *discover and then trust* a fact of the form "property path X preserves entailment for predicate P" — and that discovered fact can be wrong. This is irreducible: it can be made *data-driven* (discover transitivity from the graph + Wikidata's "transitive property" constraint and validate it) rather than *hand-coded*, which honors the spirit of your thesis, but it cannot be made *assumption-free*. The honest target is "no hand-maintained lookup tables; transitivity/relatedness discovered from Wikidata's own metadata and validated against evidence" — not "zero trusted facts about the schema." I want to flag this clearly because it's the one place where "remove all hardcoding" and "stay sound" are in genuine tension, and the resolution is *discover-and-validate*, not *eliminate*.

## 4. Blockers that require significant architectural change (consolidated)

Pulling the verdicts together, here is what *cannot* be surpassed by tuning — the places where the architecture itself must change — separated from the hard external ceilings no architecture escapes.

**Architectural cuts Aedos must make (within our control):**

| # | The cut | Why it's unavoidable | Blast radius |
|---|---|---|---|
| B1 | **Single-property → multi-property bindings** with evidence arbitration | The P31/P106 fix and all automatic predicate discovery require considering a *set* of candidate properties; a scalar `kb_property` structurally cannot | `PredicateMetadata`, DB schema, `kb_verifier.verify`, `_lookup_targets` — invasive but primitives exist |
| B2 | **Defer property selection past extraction** (carry object type into `consult`) | Predicate identity frozen at normalization before the object is seen; copula ambiguity is unresolvable downstream otherwise | Cross-layer interface change (extractor → translation oracle) |
| B3 | **Discover/verify split + bidirectional composition** (orchestrator above SPARQL stays permanent) | Cross-source chains can't be one SPARQL query; the gate-at-discovery design generates false abstains; the field abandoned BFS-frontier | Walker rewrite — significant but bounded; sources untouched |
| B4 | **Decide the retraction pillar** (minimal premise-retraction *or* remove it) | The half-built cascade is cost-without-function; the full eager TMS is the wrong model for a session-scoped verifier | Either implement a bounded provenance/delta mechanism *or* delete the machinery + §7.3 |

**Hard external ceilings (outside our control — design *around* them, don't try to beat them):**

- **KG incompleteness.** Aedos can only verify a fact Wikidata contains. For genuinely arbitrary/rare facts it cannot verify and must abstain — it converts an LLM-hallucination into an Aedos-abstention but does *not* recover the answer. "Know more than Wikidata states" is bounded by what is *derivable from present facts*, never *inventable*.
- **Multi-hop degradation past ~3 hops** is intrinsic to the task (HoVer); a better engine reaches the 60–85% band, not single-hop accuracy.
- **The provenance-vs-completeness dichotomy.** Traversal/verification gives the trace Aedos's thesis requires but hits the incompleteness wall; embedding/link-prediction beats incompleteness but gives no provenance. Aedos must stay in the traversal/provenance camp and accept the coverage ceiling — or run embedding only as a *suggest-then-verify* side channel that never reaches a user-facing verdict.
- **The ~0.85 relation-linking ceiling** on common predicates (0.48–0.65 on hard/implicit) is evidence-backed across the entire relation-linking literature.
- **Wikidata's temporal data model** — qualifiers not standalone statements, no event-relative bounds, day-max precision.
- **Aedos's own oracles hallucinate** (Xu's theorem applies to them too) — they must be treated as fallible-and-quarantined, with soundness coming from deterministic checks, cross-source agreement, or abstention, never from assuming oracle correctness.
- **The no-fine-tuning / cold-start stance costs ~10 accuracy points** versus a trained relation/path planner (RoG 85.7 vs StructGPT zero-shot 72.6 on WebQSP). This is a real, accepted cost of the cold-start commitment, not a defect.

## 5. The architecture-we-built vs architecture-we-need delta

A compact statement of the delta, subsystem by subsystem. "Change class" is the key column: it tells you where tuning helps, where engineering helps, and where only an architectural cut helps.

| Subsystem | What we built | What we need | Change class |
|---|---|---|---|
| Predicate mapping | Hardcoded normalization table + single-property oracle guess | Multi-property bindings + Wikidata-ontology-driven discovery + evidence arbitration | **Architectural cut (B1, B2)** |
| Multi-source composition | Weak BFS frontier (gate-at-discovery, depth-0 cap) + a separate working SPARQL-path mechanism in the verifier | Discover/verify split, bidirectional (premise-forward) search, SPARQL paths promoted to first-class | **Architectural cut (B3)** |
| `predicate_distribution` | Hand-seeded LLM gate at relation-family granularity | Split: KB-property transitivity (graph-discovered) + intensional kind-entailment (judgment) | Significant refactor |
| Temporal | 3 untyped string fields + `BEFORE_PRESENT` sentinel, start/end fused | Granular start/end claims + point-algebra endpoint reasoning + symbolic comparator | Bounded (T1), reuses born_on precedent |
| Claim handling | 4 silent drops + INERT filter | Designation-not-deletion: every claim → a verdict | **Bounded** |
| Correction | Flags only; corrected value computed then discarded | Emit the corrected value (minimal edit) | **Bounded (~3 edits)** |
| Retraction / TMS | Half-built eager cascade (cost without function) | Minimal premise-retraction via lazy per-claim provenance — or remove | Decision, then bounded |
| Dual-designation | Computed then collapsed (invisible) | Surface conditional verdicts — or fold to a trace flag | Bounded |
| Layer 2 routing | Dead Router/Validator | Remove, or resurrect Validator as the typed home for structural invariants | Bounded |
| Geographic | Hardcoded continent/region tables | Subsumed by property-transitivity discovery (with discover-and-validate, the one irreducible trusted-fact) | Refactor + one accepted limit |
| Python tier | Single-shot tri-state verifier | (Keep — literature-validated; make it the mandatory route for all math/counting/character claims) | No change; tighten routing |

The pattern is clear and it answers your "is the tuning lever exhausted" question architecturally: **the highest-value moves (B1–B3) are architectural cuts, not tuning.** The bounded items (verify-every-claim, correction-value, dual-designation, dead-code) are cheap wins that also happen to be the right thing regardless of the bigger cuts. There is very little left that is pure "tuning" — which confirms your instinct that the path forward is to challenge the architecture, not to keep turning knobs.

## 6. A decision framework for sequencing (deliberately non-authoritative)

You said the next step should engage, not dictate. So rather than a roadmap, here are the decisions that gate everything, in dependency order, with the trade-off each carries. These are yours to make; the rest follows.

**Decision 1 — Commit to the multi-property substrate (B1/B2)?** This is the keystone: it fixes P31/P106, kills `normalization.py`, reduces seeds toward zero, and is the prerequisite for elegant predicate discovery. Cost: a real schema + control-flow change to the verifier. *Almost everything you want in the substrate depends on this.* If yes, the design is retrieve-rerank-validate over Wikidata's own property ontology.

**Decision 2 — Adopt the discover/verify split as the composition spine (B3)?** This is how multi-hop gets unblocked without sacrificing soundness, and it's how the dormant truth-maintenance idea gets a real, bounded job (within-derivation backtracking + lazy provenance). Cost: a walker rewrite. The alternative (keep patching the BFS) is the path the whole KGQA field abandoned.

**Decision 3 — What is the temporal commitment?** T1 (granular start/end claims + endpoint arithmetic) is bounded and high-value; T2 (full interval reasoner) is probably premature; T3 (`valid_from_ref`/`valid_until_ref`) is a cheap co-change. Decide how much temporal reasoning the target use cases actually demand.

**Decision 4 — Retraction: minimal or none?** Implement the bounded premise-retraction (re-derive in-flight claims when a user changes a premise), or remove the machinery and update the spec. Not both-and-neither.

**Decision 5 — The bounded wins, independent of 1–4:** verify-every-claim (§3.4), emit-corrected-value (§3.5), dead-code resolution (§3.6). These are cheap, are right regardless, and directly improve the abstention operating point you were skeptical of. They can proceed in parallel.

Explicitly **deferred to a future version** (per your direction): Phase E model re-selection, evaluation-harness/benchmark building (including the popularity-stratified measurement that would *prove* the durable-value claim from §1–§2), and bounded calibration/seed tuning. The architecture comes first; we don't need the exact-right models or a perfect eval to make these architectural cuts — and making them will change what the eval should measure anyway.

## 7. Open questions for you

1. **The multi-property cut (Decision 1) is the keystone — do you want to commit to it now, or prototype the Wikidata-ontology discovery (P2302/P1696/P1647) on the P31/P106 case first to de-risk?** A small spike would show whether constraint-driven property selection actually resolves the copula ambiguity at acceptable cost before the full schema change.

2. **For multi-hop: SPARQL-path-first or premise-forward-first?** Both are in §3.1; SPARQL paths are the proven mechanism (fastest win for KB-internal chains), premise-forward fixes the genuinely-hard cross-source case (Williamstown/Asa). Which failure shape matters more for the target use cases?

3. **Retraction (Decision 4): is "re-derive when the user corrects a premise mid-session" a capability you want, or is single-shot verification enough?** Your "memory via walking a set of claims" comment suggests session-knowledge matters — if so, premise-retraction is part of that story; if not, we should delete the dormant machinery rather than maintain it.

4. **Do you want conditional verdicts surfaced** ("true *if* your premise holds")? This is the cheapest way to make the `*_given_assertion` distinction — your headline soundness mechanism — actually visible to a user, and it directly serves the session-knowledge use case.

5. **How literal is "every claim must be verified"?** The four `_build_claim` drops are clearly in scope. But the `INERT_PROSE` triage drop means every opinion/subjective sentence would also surface as a visible abstain (large UX blast radius). Is that desired, or should non-checkworthy be a quiet designation rather than a surfaced abstain?

6. **On the one irreducible "no hardcoding" wall (§3.7):** are you comfortable with "transitivity/relatedness *discovered from Wikidata's own metadata and validated against evidence*, but trusted once discovered" as the principled standard — accepting that the system holds *some* trusted schema facts it didn't hand-author but could in principle get wrong? This is the honest boundary of the no-hardcoding thesis.

7. **Is the 60–85% multi-hop band (the literature ceiling) acceptable as the target**, given soundness-over-coverage means the residual becomes abstentions rather than errors? If you want above that band, the only routes are KG link-prediction (sacrifices provenance — incompatible with the thesis) or a second grounding source — both are larger bets for a later version.
